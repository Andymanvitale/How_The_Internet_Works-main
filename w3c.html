W3C, HTML, CSS

World Wide Web Consortium (W3C)

The mission of the W3C is "to lead the World Wide Web to its full potential by developing protocols and guidelines that ensure the long-term growth of the Web." They believe that the web should be accessible to everyone and knowledge should be shared. They believe that people should have the ability to safely interact with one another no matter where they are in the world, and data should be stored on the web for people to view and have confidence that it will remain there.

They focus on how the web is being built and also on web development and data management. These topics are huge for obvious reasons and the work they do inspires people to get together at conferences to discuss ways that the web can be improved.

HTML/CSS

HTML stands for Hyper Text Markup Language and it is the basic visualization of a webpage. It is used to structure a webpage and provide a layout for a web developer to improve upon using other resources. One of those resources is CSS which stands for cascading style sheets. CSS is used for presenting webpages to the viewer and adds design to the layout which HTML creates. Using both together creates a more visually appealing website and can make readability easier for the viewer.

This breakdown of what the W3C believed in was created in 2005 and laid out the future of the organization. It was the basis for what they still follow today and it shows how their goals haven't changed very much. They have a mission and they are sticking to it. Their role of the development of the web has been instrumental for what has been done and what will still be accomplished.
